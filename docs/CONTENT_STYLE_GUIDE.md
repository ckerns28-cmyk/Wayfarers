# Wayfarer Content Style Guide

## Tone Lock

Wayfarer writing should be:

- concise
- mysterious but not vague
- grounded
- mythic
- not overly modern
- not exposition-heavy

## Global Writing Rules

1. Prioritize clarity first, flavor second.
2. Use short-to-medium line length in dialogue and quest text.
3. Avoid lore dumps; reveal world through implication, place, and action.
4. Keep modern slang, irony, and meta humor out of core NPC voice.
5. Let uncertainty be intentional, not confusing.

## NPC Voice Standards

### Edrin Vale

**Voice profile:** cryptic, spare, symbolic.

- Speaks in compressed lines with layered meaning.
- Uses image-rich but controlled language.
- Rarely gives complete direct answers.
- Should feel calm and observant, never frantic.

### Hunter Garran

**Voice profile:** direct, practical, wary.

- Prioritizes immediate concerns and survival logic.
- Short declarative phrasing.
- Limited ornamentation.
- Caution shows in wording, but never melodrama.

### Merchant Rowan

**Voice profile:** warm, opportunistic, grounded.

- Friendly and transactional.
- Emphasizes utility, value, and local knowledge.
- Slight persuasive tone without caricature.
- Keeps speech practical and approachable.

## Quest Text Standards

- Objective language must be explicit and actionable.
- Atmospheric flavor is secondary and brief.
- Stage transitions should always indicate what changed.
- Failure/blocked states must explain what is missing.

## Chronicle Standards

- Entries should be short, readable, useful.
- Record player-relevant outcomes, not narrative filler.
- Prefer concrete verbs and nouns.
- Avoid repeating unchanged context.

## Style Dos and Don’ts

### Do

- Use grounded fantasy diction.
- Anchor abstract ideas with concrete imagery.
- Keep mission-critical text plain.

### Don’t

- Use modern casual slang.
- Inflate stakes every line.
- Hide objectives behind poetic phrasing.
- Turn all dialogue into exposition.
